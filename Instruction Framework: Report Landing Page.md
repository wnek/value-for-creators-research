# 🌐 Instruction Framework: Creating a Webpage from Influencer Marketing Research

## 🎯 Goal
Transform a completed influencer marketing research document into a **high-quality, visually engaging webpage** that:
- Clearly presents insights and data
- Uses **attached photos as style/design reference**
- Incorporates assets from the `/assets` folder
- Emphasizes **clarity, hierarchy, and visual storytelling**

---

## 🎨 Design & Style Guidelines

### 1. Follow Visual References
- Use the **attached photos** as the primary inspiration for:
  - Layout structure
  - Typography scale
  - Color palette
  - Spacing and alignment
- Aim for a **modern, clean, and editorial style**

---

### 2. Visual Hierarchy
- Strong contrast between:
  - Headings
  - Subheadings
  - Body text
- Use:
  - Large section headers
  - Clear spacing between blocks
  - Cards or containers for grouped content

---

### 3. Section Separation (CRITICAL)
Each section from the research must be **clearly distinguished** using:
- Background color changes OR
- Section dividers OR
- Card-based layout

Ensure:
- Smooth visual flow
- Easy scannability

---

## 🧱 Page Structure

---

### 1. Hero Section
- Title of the research
- Short subtitle (1–2 lines)
- Optional:
  - Key stat
  - Visual (hero image from `/assets`)

---

### 2. Executive Summary
- 3–5 key insights
- Highlight using:
  - Bold text
  - Callout boxes
  - Icons (if available)

---

### 3. Main Sections (From Research)

Each section must:

#### Structure:
- Section title
- Short intro (1–2 sentences)
- Content blocks:
  - Paragraphs
  - Bullet points
  - Data highlights

#### Visual Enhancements:
- Use relevant images from `/assets`
- Add charts/visuals if available
- Include icons or illustrations where helpful

---

## 📊 Data Presentation

- Highlight important numbers using:
  - Large font sizes
  - Accent colors
- Use:
  - Cards
  - Inline stats
  - Tables (if needed)

Example:
- “**76% of brands increased influencer budgets in 2025**”

---

## 🧠 Solutions Sections (VERY IMPORTANT)

When presenting **industry solutions/tools/platforms**:

### Layout:
Use a **card or block per solution**

---

### Each Solution Must Include:

#### 1. Header
- Solution name
- Logo (from `/assets/logos/...` if available)

---

### 2. Summary
- Short description of the solution

---

### 3. Business Model
- Clearly labeled
- Short bullet explanation

---

### 4. Strengths & Weaknesses
- Two-column layout preferred:
  - ✅ Strengths
  - ⚠️ Weaknesses

---

### 5. Visuals
- Include:
  - Product screenshots (if available)
  - Brand visuals

---

## 🔌 Integrations Section (HIGH PRIORITY)

This section must be **visually rich and brand-focused**

### Requirements:
- Display **logos of all mentioned brands/tools**
- Use assets from:
  - `/assets/logos/`
  - `/assets/integrations/`

---

### Layout Options:
- Grid of logos
- Horizontal scroll
- Cards with:
  - Logo
  - Short description

---

### Goal:
- Make integrations **instantly recognizable**
- Prioritize **visual over text**

---

## 🖼️ Asset Usage Rules

- All images must come from:
  - `/assets/...`
- Maintain:
  - Consistent sizing
  - Proper alignment
  - High visual quality

---

## ✨ UX Enhancements

- Use:
  - Hover effects (cards, buttons)
  - Subtle animations (fade-in, slide)
- Ensure:
  - Mobile responsiveness
  - Readability across devices

---

## 📐 Layout Best Practices

- Max width: ~1100–1200px
- Use grid systems for:
  - Cards
  - Solution comparisons
- Maintain:
  - Generous whitespace
  - Consistent margins/padding

---

## ⚠️ What to Avoid

- Walls of text
- Unstructured sections
- Missing visual hierarchy
- Overuse of colors or effects
- Ignoring provided design references

---

## ✅ Final Output Requirements

The final webpage must:
- Reflect the **structure of the research**
- Be **visually engaging and easy to scan**
- Clearly separate all sections
- Highlight:
  - Key insights
  - Data points
  - Solutions
- Use **relevant images and logos**
- Present **Integrations visually and prominently**
